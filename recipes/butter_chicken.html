Butter Chicken

Description

This butter chicken recipe, or chicken makhani, is one of my favourite Indian dishes. It features a full-flavored sause that complements the chicken well. It can be made as mild or spicy as you which by adjusting the cayenne. Serve with basmati rice and naan bread.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons peanut oil, divided
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • 1/4 white onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoons ginger garlic paste
  • 2 teaspoons lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala, divided
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoons chili powder
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cup tomato puree
  • 1 cup half-and-half
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 pinch salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-size pieces
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepperm or to taste
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup water

Steps

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Saute shallot and onion until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  2. Stir in butter, ginger-garlic pastem lemon juice, 1 teaspoon garma masala, chili powder, cumin, and bay leaf. Cook and stir for 1 minute. Add tomato sauce m and cook for 2 minutes, continuing to frequently stir.
  3. Stir in half-and-half and yogurt. Reduce heat to low, and simmer for 10 minutes, frequently strring. Season with salt and pepper. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. Heat remaining 1 tablespoon oil in a large heavy skillet over medium heat. Cook chicken until lightly browned, about 10 minutes.
  5. Reduce heat, and season with remaining 1 teaspoon garam masala and cayenne. Stir in a few spoonfuls of sauce, and simmer utnil liquid has reduced, and chicken is no longer pink. Add cooked chicken into sauce and stir together.
  6. Dissolve cornstrach into water, then mix into the sauce. Cook for 5 to 10 minutes, or until thickened.
  7. Serve over rice with naans
